Aaron Franklin is a man of many talents: He’s mastered the art of brisket-based barbecue, with lines of hungry patrons to prove it; he’s helped create a one-of-a-kind food and music festival with Hot Luck; he’s a published author, sharing everything he knows about cooking meat in book form. And he’s back on bookshelves with a new entry, Franklin Steak: Dry-Aged. Live-Fired. Pure Beef., once again co-authored by Jordan Mackay. This is another deep dive into a very specific type of cooking, complete with gorgeous photography by Austin’s own Wyatt McSpadden. Franklin sat down with the Chronicle at his eponymous restaurant to talk steak.
