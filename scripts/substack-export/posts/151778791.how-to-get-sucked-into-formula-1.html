Me: Not a "car guy." Already have plenty of hobbies and sports I watch. Have seen couples in matching Ferrari outfits and balked.

Also me: Can name numerous teams, drivers, tracks, and affluvia about the worldwide sport of F1.

How did this happen? It's a step by step process in which I've willingly engaged. They are presented here.

Step 1: Randomly attend an F1 event

Last month I found myself at Circuit of the Americas (COTA) on a Friday with one ultimate goal: attend the Sting concert at the end of the night. But hey, I'm here, so let's see what this vroom-vroom is all about.

There was lots not to like: expensive and distant parking, expensive and average food, ACL-level crowds, no way to know what's going on during the race without a radio.

But I was still intrigued. The merchandising was over the top. Hordes of people wearing orange (McClaren), red (Ferrari), or black (Red Bull). And if you need some clothing of your own, plenty of temporary stores set up to sell you some.

The Sting concert was fantastic, mainly because I paid an up charge to be close to the stage. But I left thinking I'll look into this sport a little more.

Step 2: Watch an actual race

Because i was there on day one of the festivities, I only got to see some practice sessions as well as the qualifier for Saturday's sprint race. I set my recorder to get the rest of the action and experienced my first real Grand Prix races.

It's much, much better on television, at least for a novice. Again, if you have an FM radio, you can keep up with the action in the stands, but listening to British commentators take you through a race (ESPN partners with Sky Sports from the UK) is pretty sweet.

I learned a ton with that first race: how insanely fast pit stops are, how DRS is used in overtaking (also "DRS" and "overtaking"), which teams are cellar dwellers (sorry Sauber and Aston Martin), and how long a Grand Prix is. An hour and a half to go 56 laps!

Step 3: Watch "the Netflix show"

I was already aware of Drive to Survive on Netflix, and I know that reality shows are the way to build and educate a fan base. Luckily, this one is really good.

There are currently six seasons, one per year starting with 2018. Having seen a snapshot of the current set of drivers and teams, it is definitely weird to go back in time and see how things were. It's like I'm waiting for the future-now.

The personalities are of course the real hook. Other than the drivers, the big ones you'd never see from the stands are the Team Principles. This is essentially the coach or general manager. These guys are a trip. Hyper competitive, rich, and very much willing to throw barbs.

One thing I learned at this point that has helped suck me in is the scale of these operations. A team is really a mid-sized company, with any from from hundreds to a couple of thousand employees and a yearly budget of several hundred million dollars. The only product is to create two really fast cars that race a couple of dozen times a year. It's like the space race in seeing just how much engineering you can squeeze into a problem space with a large enough staff and enough money. It's kind of nuts.

Step 4: Find all the media

This and the final step are the meat and potatoes of fandom. Finding the right podcasts, news sources, and books are the bottomless pit of any hobby. Add in movies and video games (a possible future Step 6) and the itch can be continually scratched. I'll drop some recommendations here, but just know that this world is a large as you want it to be.

  • Podcast: The Race

Step 5: Watch races!

Since the local affair, I've had a chance to watch both the Mexico and Brazil Grand Prix races. From what I can tell, these three have been pretty high quality. Definitely enjoyable.

Each race is a full weekend. Practice sessions on Friday with no consequential result. Qualifying on Saturday to determine the order of the cars for the race itself. And then Sunday is go time. I'm already looking forward to 2025 with 24 tracks around the world ready to host the big show.

And next time it comes to Austin, I'll be ready. And maybe even wearing some merch.
